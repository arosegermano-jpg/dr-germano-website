New & Returning Patient Information

What to expect before, during, and after your visit with Dr. Germano.

Your Visit at a Glance

Step 1 · First Consultation

Review your history, imaging, symptoms, and goals.

Step 2 · Treatment Plan

Discuss non-surgical options, injections (including PRP), and whether surgery is appropriate.

Step 3 · Surgery & Recovery

Coordinated care through surgery, rehabilitation, and long-term joint health.

New Patients

Your first visit is an opportunity for Dr. Germano to understand your lifestyle, goals, and any prior treatments, so that together you can decide on the most appropriate plan for your hip or knee.

What to Bring

  • Photo identification and insurance card
  • Any prior imaging (X-rays, MRIs, CT scans) and reports
  • A current medication list, including doses
  • Referral documentation, if required by your insurance

What to Expect

  • Check-in with front desk and brief paperwork
  • Clinical evaluation and review of imaging
  • Time for questions about diagnosis and options
  • Discussion of next steps, including non-surgical options or surgery if indicated

You may be directed to complete hospital or practice forms through Northwell Health or Orlin & Cohen’s online patient resources.

Returning Patients

Follow-up visits focus on monitoring your progress, reviewing any changes in symptoms, and adjusting your plan as needed—whether you are considering surgery, recovering from a procedure, or continuing non-surgical care.

What to Bring

  • Any new imaging or test results completed since your last visit
  • Updated medication list or changes in medical history
  • Notes about your pain levels, mobility, or activity tolerance

What to Expect

  • Review of how you have been doing since your last visit
  • Assessment of your hip or knee function
  • Discussion of ongoing non-surgical management or timing of surgery
  • Review of recovery milestones if you are post-operative

Additional forms and resources may be accessed through Orlin & Cohen’s patient portal or Northwell Health’s patient tools.

Pre-Operative Instructions

Patients are typically provided with detailed written instructions from the hospital and practice regarding medical clearance, medications, and how to prepare your home for recovery.

For more detailed guidance, you may be directed to official joint replacement preparation resources from Northwell Health or Orlin & Cohen.

Day of Surgery

This usually includes arrival time, where to check in, when to stop eating or drinking, and what personal items to bring.

Your official hospital packet will outline check-in steps, anesthesia information, and how your family can receive updates during surgery.

Post-Operative Care & Recovery

Many patients receive written post-operative instructions and a physical therapy plan that explains what to expect in the first days, weeks, and months after surgery.

Your recovery plan may reference formal guides from Orlin & Cohen or Northwell Health describing activity progression, wound care, and when to contact the office.

Need Help?

If you have questions about your upcoming visit or surgery, the office team can help you with logistics, paperwork, and next steps.

  • View office locations & contact details
  • Visit the FAQ page
  • Orlin & Cohen patient education
  • Northwell joint replacement resources

For urgent concerns after surgery, follow the specific instructions provided in your discharge paperwork or call the on-call provider.
